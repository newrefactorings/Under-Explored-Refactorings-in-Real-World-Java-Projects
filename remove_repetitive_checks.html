Refactoring Definition:
Remove Repetitive Checking involves eliminating redundant validation or condition checks that are repeatedly performed in the same method or across similar methods, especially when the checks can be centralized or deemed unnecessary in certain contexts.

Rationale:

  • Improves Code Clarity: Removes redundant logic, making the code more concise and easier to read.
  • Enhances Maintainability: Centralizing checks or eliminating unnecessary ones reduces duplication, simplifying future changes.
  • Avoids Logical Redundancy: Prevents redundant validations that may not contribute additional value, streamlining method functionality.
