Refactoring Definition:
Remove Overriding Method that Equals to its Overridden Method involves deleting an overriding method in a subclass when it is identical to or redundant with the behavior of the method in the superclass.

Rationale:

  • Eliminates Redundancy: Avoids duplicating code that already exists and is inherited from the superclass, adhering to the DRY (Don't Repeat Yourself) principle.
  • Improves Maintainability: Reduces clutter in the codebase, making it easier to understand and maintain.
  • Minimizes Risk of Errors: Prevents the possibility of divergence between the overridden and inherited method in future updates.

Note: This is a context-specific refactoring applied when an inherited method in a subclass doesn't provide any additional functionality beyond what is already provided by the superclass. This is common in object-oriented design when the subclass does not need to alter the behavior of the method from the parent class.
